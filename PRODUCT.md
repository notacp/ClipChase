# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

YouTube power users, researchers, and heavy content consumers who need to find specific moments across a channel's catalog. They're searching while actively watching or researching — mid-task, intent-driven, no patience for friction. They're comfortable with browser extensions and expect tool-quality precision, not consumer-app hand-holding.

## Product Purpose

ClipChase is a Chrome extension that lets you search any YouTube channel by phrase and get every video that mentions it with exact, clickable timestamps. It eliminates the "I know someone said this somewhere" frustration of navigating large video catalogs. Success means a user installs the extension, runs their first search, and immediately finds the moment they were looking for.

The landing page's only job is to drive that install. Copy and design must communicate utility fast and create enough confidence to click.

## Brand Personality

Playful, direct, snappy. Irreverent about the YouTube experience without being dismissive of it. Copy moves fast — short sentences, no hedging, no corporate softening. The personality lives in the writing as much as in the visual.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages: gradient CTAs, glassmorphism cards, hero metric blocks with big numbers and small labels
- Gradient text anywhere
- Identical card grids with icon + heading + description
- All-caps eyebrow labels over every section
- Numbered section markers (01/02/03) as pure scaffolding
- Side-stripe borders on cards or callouts
- Cream/sand/beige near-white backgrounds as a "warmth" default
- Any design where the template is visible — the AI scaffold should be invisible

## Design Principles

1. **Utility is the personality.** The interface should feel like a well-made tool, not a marketing brochure about a tool. Show the product doing its job; don't describe it.
2. **Every word earns its place.** Short copy is a feature. No section headings that restate what follows. No subheads that exist to fill whitespace.
3. **Snappy means fast.** Transitions, layout rhythm, and section pacing all signal speed. If something feels slow to scroll through, it's too slow.
4. **Earned confidence, not generic polish.** Precision over decoration. The design should feel like it was made by someone who actually uses YouTube constantly, not by a brand team.
5. **Playfulness is in the details.** Not mascots or bubbly shapes — specific word choices, unexpected micro-copy, motion that has character.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large/bold text and UI components. Reduced motion support required for all animations. Keyboard navigation for interactive elements.
